What is a Word Counter?

A word counter online is an essential utility for writers, developers, and SEO specialists that analyzes a text string to determine its length and density. Beyond a simple tally of characters, a professional-grade word counter provides insights into the structure of the document—calculating sentences, paragraphs, and reading time based on linguistic averages. It is used to meet strict character limits for social media, academic essays, or metadata fields.

ZeroTools provides a "Terminal Minimal" text analyzer that processes your data entirely within your browser's RAM. Whether you are checking the keyword density of a blog post or the length of a README file, your content is never sent to our servers. This ensures that sensitive drafts and proprietary documentation remain strictly confidential.

How to use the Word Counter

  1. Paste your text into the primary input box or start typing manually.
  2. Observe the real-time statistics update in the grid at the top of the page.
  3. Enter a keyword in the "Check Keyword" field to calculate its frequency and density percentage within your text.
  4. Set a "Char Limit" if you are writing for a platform with strict constraints (like Twitter or meta descriptions) to see a visual progress bar.
  5. Review the "Top 10 Word Frequency" table to identify repetitive vocabulary and improve the quality of your writing.

FAQ

How is reading time calculated?

We use an industry-standard average reading speed of 238 words per minute (WPM). This provides a realistic estimate for the time it takes an average adult to consume your content silently. Speaking time is calculated at a slower pace of 130 WPM, which is the typical speed for conversational speech.

Are spaces included in the character count?

Yes. By default, the "Characters" stat includes every byte in the text, including spaces, tabs, and newlines. However, we also provide a "No Spaces" metric for specific use cases where you only need the count of the actual letters and punctuation marks.

Does this tool save my text?

ZeroTools uses `localStorage` to save your last input locally in your browser so you don't lose work if the page is refreshed[cite: 1, 2]. We have no backend server, database, or analytics that capture the actual text you input. Your data remains in your control at all times.
